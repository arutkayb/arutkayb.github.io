## Day Overview

**11 sessions across 3 projects · 287 messages**

A day of investigative work disguised as a stability sprint. The real driver was the ongoing IAP revenue mystery in AdvancedSCalendar — Crashlytics triage was an attempt to rule out bugs as the culprit, and the AdMob migration was a frustration-driven pivot after seeing StartApp earn $5/month despite high impressions. Neither effort resolved the core question, but the onboarding funnel data (40% drop at signup, 70% of new users never adding data) emerged as the sharper lead.

**AdvancedSCalendar**: Triaged 20 Crashlytics issues, shipped 4 fix PRs (~163 crash events), migrated ad SDK from StartApp to AdMob, and analyzed monetization gating.
**dear-diary**: Ran the March 28 diary review.
**advancedscalendar tools**: Quick analysis of current monetization model and feature gates.

---

## What I Worked On

- **AdvancedSCalendar — Crashlytics triage & fixes** — Fetched top 20 crash/ANR reports from the last 2 weeks, created 9 fix branches. Shipped PRs #58 (analytics NPE cluster, 97 events), #60 (login null safety, 38 events), #61 (fragment lifecycle, 21 events), #62 (billing null guard, 7 events). Root causes were straightforward: initialization ordering, process death state restoration, fragment detach races. All simple null checks and edge case guards.
- **AdvancedSCalendar — FirebaseUI revert** — PR #59 attempted to fix the largest crash cluster (120 events) with `FLAG_ACTIVITY_NO_HISTORY`, but it broke the email sign-in flow entirely. Reverted and parked — not considered a high-impact user-facing bug.
- **AdvancedSCalendar — StartApp to AdMob migration** — Replaced StartApp SDK with Google AdMob (v25.1.0). Created `AdMobAdFactory` implementing existing `AdFactory` interface for a clean swap. Fixed interstitial timing bug and duplicate banner stacking during integration. Motivated by StartApp earning only ~$5/month despite high engagement — AdMob expected to generate ~20x more.
- **AdvancedSCalendar — Onboarding funnel analysis** — Identified critical drop-offs: 40% of users leave at welcome-to-signup, and 70% of those who sign up never add any data. Only ~18% of new users actually engage with the app. This is now the leading hypothesis for poor IAP conversion alongside the audience shift from recent ASO changes.
- **AdvancedSCalendar — Monetization model review** — Analyzed current gating structure (PIN Lock, Remove Ads, Unlimited Partners, Unlimited Photos, bundles). Identified data loss bug during privacy policy updates as a critical blocker before pursuing new monetization strategies.
- **dear-diary** — Ran March 28 diary review covering Signal Scout pivot realization, AdvancedSCalendar billing investigation, and content publishing.

---

## Reflections

### Wins
- Shipped 4 PRs eliminating ~163 crash events — even if the motivation was IAP investigation, the app is measurably more stable now.
- Completed the StartApp-to-AdMob migration cleanly using the existing `AdFactory` interface — no call site changes needed.
- Wrote and published a Medium post, which felt like a genuine accomplishment in a day that otherwise lacked shipped user-facing work.

### Learnings
- The crash fixes were all simple null checks and edge cases — the IAP problem isn't a bug problem. The real issue lives in the onboarding funnel: only 18% of new users reach meaningful engagement.
- StartApp at $5/month with high impressions confirms that ad network choice matters enormously — not all fill rates and eCPMs are equal.
- Ruling things out is progress, even when it doesn't feel like it. Eliminating "bugs causing IAP drops" as a hypothesis narrows the search.

### Challenges
- The day felt unproductive despite 17 commits and 4 shipped PRs — no release went out to users and the core IAP question remains open.
- The FirebaseUI crash cluster (120 events) is parked without a fix. The attempted solution broke a critical user flow, and there's no clear alternative yet.

### Patterns
- The day was methodical and sequential (triage → branch → fix → review → ship → next), which is efficient for clearing a backlog but doesn't leave room for the higher-leverage thinking the IAP problem needs.
- Frustration as a catalyst: the AdMob migration wasn't planned but happened because $5/month was annoying enough to act on immediately. Sometimes that's the right call.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. When users aren't buying, check the funnel before the features — 70% of new users never adding data means they never reach the paywall.
2. A day of 17 commits and 4 merged PRs can still feel unproductive if none of it ships to users — shipping to production is what closes the loop.
3. Eliminating hypotheses (bugs aren't causing the IAP drop) is real progress, even when it feels like spinning wheels.
4. Ad network economics vary wildly — switching from StartApp to AdMob with the same traffic could mean the difference between $5/month and $100/month.
