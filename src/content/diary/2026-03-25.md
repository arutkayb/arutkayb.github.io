## Reflections

### Wins
- Identified and shipped three production bug fixes in a single day, all triggered by noticing funnel degradation in Google Analytics — not user reports, not crash logs.
- The platinum-only pricing experiment went from plan to merged PR in under 3 hours, following the established 4-file remote config pattern cleanly.
- Eliminated the `.claude/` permission friction that had been a recurring annoyance — a structural fix, not a workaround.
- The signal-scout pipeline ran at scale across 75 sessions autonomously, processing 15+ apps without manual intervention.

### Learnings
- Sales numbers are the most reliable signal for silent bugs — race conditions and process failures don't crash the app, they just quietly break the user journey. Crashlytics alone isn't enough.
- Claude Code treats `.claude/` as a protected path category by design. Normal allow rules don't override it; it required either a PreToolUse hook or a folder structure change.
- Server-side fixes (cloud functions) are preferable for purchase verification bugs — no app update needed, all users benefit immediately.
- Post-plan iteration should be treated as legitimate refinement, not scope creep. Flagging every change after plan completion creates friction without adding value.

### Challenges
- The `.claude/` to `agent-logs/` migration was more involved than expected (2+ hours) due to mid-migration sub-agent path confusion and 14 files needing coordinated updates.
- Mental load from parallel context-switching across AdvancedSCalendar, agentic-files, and my_next_niche. Focus degrades over the course of the day, and the tendency to skip careful review increases.
- Tendency to rely more heavily on agentic skills as mental fatigue sets in — actively modifying skills to compensate by prompting for potentially missed points, but recognizing this as an evolving tension.

### Patterns
- Agentic parallel workflows are a net positive for throughput but come with a real cognitive cost. The productivity bump is worth it today, but the clumsy feeling and declining focus over a full day of context-switching is a signal to watch.
- The day followed a reactive-then-proactive arc: morning started with bug discovery and firefighting (funnel analysis → fixes), then shifted to intentional work (feature experiment, tooling overhaul, market research).
- Skill refinement is becoming a meta-habit: improving the tools that improve the workflow. This compounds but also adds another layer of context-switching.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Your funnel is a bug detector. Silent race conditions and process failures don't show up in crash logs — they show up as missing sales. Make analytics review a regular debugging practice, not just a growth metric.
2. When agentic workflows let you run 94 sessions in a day, the bottleneck shifts from execution to attention. The skills can compensate for declining focus, but only if you keep refining them to catch what tired-you would miss.
3. Structural fixes beat workarounds. Moving from `.claude/` to `agent-logs/` was a 2-hour investment that permanently eliminates a daily friction point — the kind of work that's easy to defer but compounds when done.
4. Shipping a pricing experiment from idea to production in one day is possible when you have established patterns (remote config 4-file flow) and a review-execute pipeline. The pattern is the accelerator.
