## Reflections

### Wins
- Shipped 3 AdvancedSCalendar releases covering billing fixes, localization, and compliance — productive even if the cadence isn't ideal
- dear-diary is now open source with CI, proper docs, and accurate extraction after catching and fixing 3 significant data bugs through dogfooding
- The agent curation session crystallized a clear mental model: skills teach workflows, agents bring domain thinking — and knowing when each applies

### Learnings
- Skills and agents solve different problems: a skill tells a generic agent *how* to do something; an agent persona brings domain knowledge, best practices, and a way of thinking you can't encode in instructions. Engineering agents clash with established workflows, but advisory agents (growth, marketing) fill genuine knowledge gaps.
- Dogfooding dear-diary surfaced extraction bugs that tests alone wouldn't have caught — reading your own output carefully is an underrated QA technique
- Material library version compatibility with FirebaseUI is a minefield: 1.12.0 broke ThemeEnforcement, 1.6.1 was the sweet spot, and concrete `@color/` references beat attribute references in cross-activity contexts

### Challenges
- In-app purchase revenue has dropped to near-zero for a week — likely a combination of adding subscription alternatives, price increases, and drastic Play Store description changes. Still diagnosing which factor is dominant.
- Three releases in one day isn't great for users — the ad-hoc fix cycle reflects limited planning time as a solo developer, and batching would be better

### Patterns
- Heavy context-switching across 8 projects, but most work concentrated in AdvancedSCalendar (18/37 sessions) — the long tail of small touches across other projects adds up
- A recurring build-then-strategize rhythm: coding sessions on AdvancedSCalendar interleaved with growth/marketing thinking (ASO, ad pitch, blog post)
- Spending time on tooling (agent curation, dear-diary improvements) that pays forward but competes with direct product work

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Three releases in a day means the process needs batching — ship when ready, but group fixes into weekly releases instead of pushing each one immediately.
2. Advisory agent personas fill knowledge gaps that workflow skills can't — growth hacking, marketing, and design benefit from a domain thinker, not just instructions.
3. Dogfooding your own tools is the fastest way to find real bugs — reading dear-diary output carefully caught 3 extraction issues that unit tests missed.
4. The in-app purchase revenue drop needs focused investigation — too many variables changed at once (subscriptions, pricing, ASO copy) to know what's hurting.
5. Solo development means wearing every hat, but the growth/marketing work is no longer optional — it's where the leverage is now that the product is mature.
